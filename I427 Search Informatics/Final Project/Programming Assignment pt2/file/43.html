Student Organizations

Level up

Our 20+ student organizations will take your Luddy experience to new heights.

Bond with classmates. Hone your skills. Grow as a leader. Enjoy exclusive opportunities like behind-the-scenes visits to businesses. And get face time with CEOs and other executives, who partner with our organizations specifically to meet students like you.

Find organizations

This page lists Luddy’s organizations for undergraduate and graduate students and organizations for graduate students only.

To start a new Luddy organization, contact Tiana Iruoje at tiwillia@indiana.edu.

You can also explore all 750+ IU student organizations through beINvolved.

Serve IT nonprofit clinic

Get professional experience while doing good. In the Serve IT clinic, students help local nonprofits with their information technology needs, from providing tech support to building databases.

Learn more about the Serve IT clinic

Organizations for undergrad and graduate students

Computer Science Club

Expand your computing skills, make friends, network, and seize career-related opportunities.

Cyber Security Club

Learn about and raise awareness of cyber security through workshops and events with professionals.

Data Science Club

Enhance your skills, participate in hackathons and datathons, and network with alumni and professionals.

Ideation and Creation Entrepreneurs (ICE)

Advance your idea as a student innovator.

Informatics and Computing Student Association (ICSA)

Develop programs and services for current Luddy students, including an annual career fair.

INgineering

Build your engineering knowledge and skills through projects, guest lectures, and competitions.

National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE)

Become a successful, culturally responsible Black engineerâ€”and help others become the same.

Nothing Is Binary

Get together with Luddy students, faculty, and staff who identify as GLBTQ or as Allies.

Product Management Club

Explore the field of product management and prepare for a career in it.

Programmatik

Practice for programming interviews and solve interesting programming problems.

Luddy Consulting Association (LCA)

Explore the world of consulting and develop key consulting skills.

Sociotechnical Ethics Society

Consider ethics as they relate to sociotechnical developments.

Student Alumni Council

Engage students and young alumni through fundraising and social events.

Women in Computing (WIC)

Encourage and celebrate women in technology, and join a community of strong, driven, and talented women.

Spread the word about Luddy

Undergrads, tell high school students, parents, and current IU students about life at Luddy as a Student Ambassador or a Pacesetter.

Also, Luddy undergrads and graduate students can join the Social Media Team to share information about our school and events. Write to icsocial@indiana.edu.

Organizations for graduate students only

American Library Association, Student Chapter

Work on events that support library science students in their academic and professional goals.

Graduate Informatics Student Association

Serve as a liaison between students and the department, and organize social events.

Progressive Librarians Guild, Student Chapter

Promote socially responsible librarianship, and further the vital role libraries play in a democratic society.

Society of American Archivists, Student Chapter

Explore archiving alongside Master of Library Science students and other archive enthusiasts.

Society of Art Librarianship

Delve into the field of art librarianship through professional development, networking, informational talks, and visits to fine arts libraries

Special Libraries Association

Learn about atypical applications of librarianship in such areas as law, medicine, business, museums, special collections, and more.

Students of Music Librarianship Group

Develop new skills and connect with others who are interested in music and performing arts librarianship.
