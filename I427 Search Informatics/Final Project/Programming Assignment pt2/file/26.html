Degrees for the professionals and researchers of tomorrow

Master’s degrees

M.S. in Bioinformatics

Learn to model, analyze, and manage massive amounts of biological data in a program that combines computing, informatics, biology, and statistics.

M.S. in Computer Science

Master the field of computer science in this technical, highly marketable degree that prepares you for a job in Silicon Valley and beyond.

M.S. in Data Science—Residential

A focus on foundational knowledge will help lay the groundwork for a lifelong career as a data specialist, while plenty of hands-on training will give you a competitive edge in today’s job market.

M.S. in Data Science—Online

If you want to advance your career and increase your earning potential, but returning to school full time isn’t an option, the online M.S. in Data Science may be right for you.

M.S. in Human-Computer Interaction Design

Go beyond just using technology to solve a problem—learn how to create a technology experience that will be functional, intuitive, and user friendly.

M.S. in Informatics

Integrate your technical skills and computer science methods with an array of disciplines as you learn about the technological, social, and scientific effects of information technology.

Master of Information Science

Prepare for a future designing, developing, programming, and managing the technology we use every day to store, access, and transfer knowledge.

M.S. in Intelligent Systems Engineering

Learn to use computational engineering and artificial intelligence to find solutions for an ever-changing world. You will prepare for a Ph.D. program or a variety of high-demand careers.

Master of Library Science

Learn how to evaluate, preserve, collect, and organize information within one of the world’s largest and best academic library systems.

M.S. in Secure Computing

Get ready to protect our technology and understand the people who would do harm. This degree offers a highly technical, multifaceted approach to cybersecurity.

College of Arts and Sciences degrees

These programs are administered and conferred from IU's College of Arts and Sciences.

M.S. in Applied Statistics

Complement your IU Ph.D. studies with a secondary degree in statistics from the College of Arts and Sciences, focusing on data analysis, statistical computing, and consulting with real-world clients.

M.S. in Statistical Science

Offered through the College of Arts and Sciences, this four-semester program prepares students for careers in statistics and data science through data analysis, statistical computing, and consulting with real-world clients.

Accelerated master’s and 4 + 1 master's programs

Current undergraduates can apply to these programs to earn another degree in as little as one year.

Explore our accelerated master’s and 4 + 1 programs

Dual degrees

Information and Library Science dual degrees

Earn two different master’s degrees in less time and with fewer credits. Choose from 20 pairings and a wide range of disciplines.

Dual M.S. in Secure Computing and M.A. in Russian and East European Studies

Combine cybersecurity skills with expertise in Russia and Eastern Europe.

An interdisciplinary cybersecurity degree

In addition to the M.S. in Secure Computing, we offer an M.S. in Cybersecurity Risk Management, which teaches you the technical, legal, and business aspects of cybersecurity so you can protect information and mitigate threats. This program is a partnership among Luddy, the IU Kelley School of Business, and the IU Maurer School of Law.

Learn about the M.S. in Cybersecurity Risk Management

Specialist in Library and Information Science

This post-master’s degree is ideal for working professionals. It’s in-depth yet flexible and can be personalized to fit your interests and goals.

Learn about the Specialist in Library and Information Science

Ph.D. degrees

Ph.D. in Computer Science

Explore new frontiers in computing through theoretical and practical research. You will be prepared to work in industry, research labs, or academia.

Ph.D. in Informatics

Conduct practical and theoretical research into areas such as complex networks and systems, human-computer interaction design, and virtual heritage.

Ph.D. in Information Science

Join the next generation of information scientists. You’ll conduct original research and collaborate with some of the field’s biggest names.

Ph.D. in Intelligent Systems Engineering

Conduct research and advance the evolution of engineering using artificial intelligence, embedded computing components, and sophisticated data interpretation.

Ph.D. in Statistical Science

Prepare for a career in statistical theory and application through this rigorous, research-oriented program offered through the College of Arts and Sciences.

Ph.D. degrees

Ph.D. Minors

  • Animal Computer Interaction
  • Bioengineering
  • Bioinformatics
  • Complex networks and systems
  • Computer engineering
  • Computer science
  • Cyber-physical systems
  • Data science
  • Human-Computer Interaction/Design
  • Informatics
  • Information science
  • Molecular and nanoscale engineering
  • Neuroengineering
  • Security informatics
  • Social informatics
  • Statistical science

Graduate certificates

Graduate Certificate in Data Science—Online

The data science certificate is completed 100 percent online, and can be earned in as few as two semesters—giving you the flexibility to build a schedule of courses that suits your lifestyle.

Graduate Certificate in Information Architecture

Start down a new career path or expand the one you’re already on by becoming a qualified and certified information architect.

Graduate Certificate in Cybersecurity

Learn to design, implement, and manage secure information technology systems.
