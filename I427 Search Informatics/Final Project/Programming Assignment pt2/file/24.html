Academics

Major in the future

Our programs range from the super technical to the highly applied, and many can be personalized. Because everyone needs technology skills, our degrees, minors, and certificates are great additions to any area of study.

You’ll get ready for in-demand careers and prepare to use technology and information to solve big problems, improve lives, and shape tomorrow.

You can pursue whatever interests you, whether it’s preserving knowledge or developing artificial intelligence, designing wearable technology or analyzing and applying the information it gathers.

Bertolt Sobolik

M.S. in Data Science—Residential, starting as an M.S. in Data Science—Online student

Today

Wiring together credit card–sized computers to create a computer cluster, a high-performance unit that maximizes database storage and processing power

Tomorrow

Processing large data sets, building infrastructure, and developing natural language processing models as a data engineer in the music and media industry

Learn about the Data Science Program

Ingenuity thrives here

We’re always making discoveries, inventing disciplines, and pushing the limits of how we use technology and information.

Collaboration is key to our creativity. You can work with computer scientists, intelligent systems engineers, informaticists, data scientists, librarians, information scientists, statisticians, visualization experts, biologists—anyone who will help you produce inspired work.

  • Research
  • Faculty and their research interests

A “let’s do it” culture

We make things happen. Luddy students get ideas—for projects or research or student organizations or startups—and bring them to life and sometimes even get paid for them.

  • Academic opportunities
  • Innovation and Entrepreneurship Program

Signs you belong at Luddy: you love whiteboarding, late-night programming and design sessions, side passion projects, and helping people—and you’re always skeptical of the words “it can’t be done.”

Well-rounded professionals and researchers

Tomorrow’s careers call for more than field-specific knowledge—they require an ability to apply ideas across areas to find creative solutions. By studying at a technology school in a renowned and wide-ranging public university, you’ll get depth in your field and opportunities for breadth in the disciplines you explore, from the humanities to business.

Luddy advisors: like a map app for your education

Your advisor will help you find the best way to reach your goals, however off-the-beaten-path they may seem.
